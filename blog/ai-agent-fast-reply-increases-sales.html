Research from Harvard Business Review and multiple sales analytics firms has consistently found that responding to a customer inquiry within five minutes makes a business 100 times more likely to qualify that lead compared to responding after 30 minutes. Why does response time have such a dramatic effect? Several interconnected reasons:

Purchase intent has a short half-life

When a customer sends a message asking about your product, they are in a moment of active interest. That interest is at its peak right when they send the message. As time passes without a response, two things happen: the emotional momentum of the interest fades, and they start looking elsewhere. Other sellers, other brands, other options. By the time you reply an hour later, the decision window may have already closed.

Speed signals competence and reliability

A fast response communicates something beyond the answer itself. It says: "We are attentive, organized, and professional." A slow response communicates the opposite, even if the eventual response is excellent. Customers draw conclusions about what it would be like to actually be a customer — how responsive you will be when they need post-purchase support — based on how responsive you are before the sale.

Comparison shopping happens in real time

Most consumers looking to buy something are not limiting their inquiry to one business. They are messaging multiple options simultaneously. The business that responds first has a clear advantage: they start the relationship, establish the frame of comparison, and often close the sale before slower competitors have even replied.
