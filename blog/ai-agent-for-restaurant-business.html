Restaurants receive more repetitive questions than almost any other type of business. Day after day, it's the same messages: "What's on the menu today?", "Do you have vegetarian options?", "How much is delivery?", "Can I make a reservation for Saturday night?", "Are you open on holidays?"

Answering these takes time you don't have — especially during service hours when the restaurant demands your full attention. Many restaurants end up with a backlog of unanswered messages, which means lost bookings and frustrated customers.

What an AI Agent Handles for Restaurants

A well-configured AI agent covers virtually all routine restaurant inquiries:

  • Menu questions: Dishes, prices, portion sizes, ingredients, allergens
  • Dietary options: Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher
  • Hours and location: Opening times, address, parking, accessibility
  • Delivery and takeaway: Whether you deliver, delivery radius, minimum order, delivery time
  • Reservation requests: Collecting date, time, party size, and contact information
  • Special events: Private dining, catering, birthday packages
  • Daily specials: If you update the knowledge base with daily information

The Reservation Flow Example

Here's how an AI agent handles a reservation inquiry naturally:

Customer: "Hi, I'd like to book a table for this Saturday"

AI Agent: "We'd love to have you! For Saturday, we have availability at 7pm and 8:30pm. How many people will be joining you?"

Customer: "4 people, 7pm please"

AI Agent: "Perfect! I've noted a table for 4 at 7pm this Saturday. Could I get your name and a contact number so we can confirm your reservation?"

This entire exchange happens automatically, even at 11pm when you're closed and the kitchen staff has gone home. The reservation details come to you in the morning, ready to action.

Handling Allergen and Dietary Questions

This is an area where accuracy matters enormously. Customers with serious allergies need reliable information. The right approach is to train your AI agent with accurate, specific information about your menu items and their ingredients — and configure it to be cautious: "Our [dish] contains nuts. For customers with nut allergies, please confirm with our kitchen before ordering."

An AI agent that gives accurate allergen information builds trust. One that guesses gets people sick. Your knowledge base setup is critical here.

Setting Up Your Restaurant's AI Agent

The setup is straightforward. You provide:

  1. Your menu with prices and descriptions
  2. Allergen and dietary information
  3. Operating hours and location
  4. Delivery policy (if applicable)
  5. Reservation process (how you take bookings)
  6. Any special events or promotions

Once this is in the knowledge base, your AI employee is ready to work. It represents your restaurant accurately and professionally, answering the questions your customers actually ask.
