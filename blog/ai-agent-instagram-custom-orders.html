When someone DMs you asking for a custom order, the conversation typically involves many steps: understanding what they want, checking whether it is feasible, discussing options and variations, confirming specifications, agreeing on a price, collecting a deposit, and establishing a delivery timeline. Each of these steps requires a response, and each response requires your involvement if you are doing it manually.

For busy artisans, designers, or any maker who does custom work, the intake process can consume hours every day — hours that are not spent actually making things. An AI agent can handle the entire intake process, collecting all the necessary information and presenting you with a complete brief so you can focus on the creative work.

Designing Your Custom Order Intake Flow

The first step is thinking through exactly what information you need to produce a custom order. This varies by product type, but the general categories are:

  • Product type and specifications: What exactly does the customer want? What dimensions, materials, styles, or features are they envisioning?
  • Personalization details: Names, dates, messages, initials, photos — whatever makes the piece unique to them
  • Quantity: Is this a single piece or multiple units?
  • Deadline: When does the customer need it by? Do they have a hard deadline (a birthday, a wedding, an event)?
  • Budget: What range are they working within? Or would they prefer you quote based on their specifications?
  • Reference materials: Do they have examples, inspiration photos, or specific requirements they want to share?

Once you have this list, your AI agent can collect all of it naturally through conversation — asking one or two questions at a time in a friendly, exploratory way rather than presenting a bureaucratic intake form.

Handling Pricing Conversations for Custom Work

Pricing custom orders is one of the most uncomfortable conversations many small business owners have. Customers often ask for a price before fully understanding what they want, which makes accurate quoting impossible. An AI agent handles this elegantly by explaining that pricing depends on the specific requirements and walking the customer through the specification process first.

"Great idea! To give you an accurate quote, I just need a few details about what you're envisioning. Let's start with the basics..." This approach positions the pricing conversation as a collaborative process rather than a negotiation, which sets a much more positive tone.

Once the agent has gathered all specifications, it can either provide a quote directly (if your pricing is formula-based) or flag the conversation with a complete brief for your review and pricing decision. You spend your mental energy on pricing decisions, not on gathering basic information.

Managing Customer Expectations on Custom Orders

One of the most common sources of custom order complaints is misaligned expectations around timeline and process. Customers who do not know what to expect become anxious and send repeated "when will it be ready?" messages. An AI agent sets clear expectations at the beginning of the process: when production starts, what the typical timeline is, how and when they will receive updates, and what happens if their deadline cannot be met.

This proactive communication dramatically reduces the anxiety-driven follow-up messages that interrupt your work. The customer feels informed and confident, and you are free to focus on production rather than status updates.

Collecting Deposits for Custom Work

Custom work almost always requires a deposit to secure a spot in your production schedule and cover material costs. Your AI agent can communicate your deposit policy clearly, explain why it is required, and provide the payment instructions — all as a natural part of the intake conversation rather than an awkward afterthought.

"To confirm your order and reserve your spot in our production queue, we require a 50% deposit. Here's how to send it..." This framing positions the deposit as a professional practice rather than a sign of distrust, which most customers understand and appreciate.
