How an AI Agent Answers Price Questions on Instagram

🗓 March 23, 2026⏱ 8 min read

"How much does this cost?" is probably the most common question any product-based business receives on Instagram. It shows up in comments, in DMs, in Story replies, and sometimes just as a lone question mark under a post. It is also the question that, if answered well and quickly, most reliably leads to a sale.

The challenge is that price questions are not always simple to answer. There may be multiple variants at different price points. Shipping costs might vary by location. There might be bundle deals, discounts for returning customers, or wholesale pricing for bulk orders. Answering well requires both speed and accuracy.

An AI agent handles price questions exactly the way your best salesperson would — with immediate, confident, complete answers that move the conversation toward a purchase.

Instagram is primarily a discovery platform. People find products they did not know they were looking for, become interested, and immediately want to know if the price fits their budget before investing more emotional energy in the product. The price question is essentially a qualifying question: "Is this even worth my consideration?"

If the answer comes quickly and confidently, the customer moves forward. If there is no answer, they assume the price might be too high, or that you are not responsive, and they move on. If the answer comes hours later, the moment of interest has passed.

An AI agent answers within seconds. That alone — pure response speed — is a major conversion advantage.

The Three Types of Price Inquiries

Not all price questions are the same, and a well-configured AI agent handles each type appropriately:

Direct price requests

"How much is this?" or "What's the price?" — these are straightforward. The AI agent responds with the price, possibly with a brief mention of what is included (free shipping, warranty, bundled items) to reinforce value. It then invites the person to proceed with a purchase or ask additional questions.

Comparative or budget questions

"Is this cheaper than [competitor]?" or "Do you have anything under $50?" — these require the AI agent to understand your full product range and make appropriate recommendations. Configure the AI agent with your complete catalog and let it suggest the most relevant option for the customer's stated budget.

Negotiation attempts

"Can you do it cheaper?" or "I'll buy two if you give me a discount" — these are delicate. Your AI agent should be configured with clear rules about your discount policy. If you never discount, the AI agent politely explains your pricing and reinforces value. If you offer bundle discounts, it explains those. If it's outside the AI agent's authority to offer discounts, it can flag the conversation for your personal follow-up.

Public Comments vs. Private DMs for Price Answers

There is an important strategic decision when someone asks about price in a public comment: do you answer publicly or redirect to DMs? Both have advantages.

Answering publicly helps everyone else who is considering the product and wonders the same thing. It shows you are transparent and responsive. It can actually increase conversion when the price-to-value ratio is strong.

Redirecting to DMs allows for a more personalized conversation. It gives you privacy to discuss bundles, volume pricing, or any deals without setting public expectations. It also moves the customer into a higher-converting one-on-one setting.

A practical approach: have your AI agent give a general price range publicly ("Our handbags start at $45 — I'll send you the full catalog in DMs!") and then follow up with complete pricing information in a DM. This satisfies the public audience while setting up a private purchase conversation.

Handling "Why Is It So Expensive?" Gracefully

Price objections are a normal part of selling. When someone questions your pricing, the worst response is defensiveness or silence. The best response addresses the objection by reinforcing value.

Your AI agent can be configured to respond to price objections with clear, compelling value statements: what the product is made of, how long it lasts, what is included in the price, what makes it different from cheaper alternatives. These responses do not need to be defensive — they simply need to be informative and confident.

A well-written value response to a price objection can actually strengthen purchase intent rather than losing the customer. Someone who questioned the price and received a persuasive explanation of value often becomes a more committed buyer than someone who never questioned it at all.

The Bottom Line on Price Questions

Price questions are opportunities, not obstacles. A customer who asks about price is a customer who is interested. Give them a fast, complete, confident answer — with context that reinforces value — and you will convert a significant portion of those inquiries into sales.
