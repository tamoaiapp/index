Business buyers often do not announce themselves explicitly. They ask questions that signal B2B intent, but the phrasing might not be immediately obvious. Signs that a DM is from a business buyer rather than a retail customer include:

  • Questions about minimum order quantities ("Do you have a minimum order?")
  • Price-per-unit questions at scale ("What's the price if I order 50 units?")
  • Questions about customization or private labeling ("Can you put my logo on this?")
  • Requests for product samples
  • Questions about invoices, payment terms, or net-30 arrangements
  • Mentions of being a store, a salon, a boutique, or another business type

Your AI agent should be configured to recognize these signals and shift into a B2B conversation mode when it detects them, rather than treating the inquiry as a standard retail customer interaction.

What Your AI Agent Needs to Know About Your Wholesale Program

To handle B2B inquiries effectively, your AI agent needs clear information about your wholesale offering. Before deploying it for this purpose, configure it with:

  • Minimum order quantities for wholesale pricing
  • Wholesale price tiers (e.g., 10+ units, 50+ units, 100+ units)
  • Lead times for large orders
  • Available customization options and any additional costs
  • Sample policy (free, paid, credited against first order)
  • Payment terms you accept
  • Any restrictions on who you sell wholesale to (geography, business type, exclusivity)

With this information, your AI agent can give potential wholesale buyers a complete, professional overview of your program — the kind of information that used to require a 30-minute phone call.

The B2B Qualification Conversation

B2B deals often take longer to close than retail sales and involve more consideration. Your AI agent should be configured to move through a qualification conversation that identifies whether the prospect is a genuine wholesale buyer and what their specific needs are.

A well-designed B2B qualification flow might include:

  1. Confirming they are a business buyer and what type of business they run
  2. Understanding the approximate volume they are looking for
  3. Providing wholesale pricing information based on their volume needs
  4. Answering product-specific questions
  5. Offering to send a sample or arrange a more detailed discussion
  6. Collecting their business contact details for follow-up

This flow can happen entirely within Instagram DMs, handled automatically by your AI agent. By the time you review the conversation, the prospect is pre-qualified, their needs are documented, and the groundwork for a wholesale relationship has been laid.

Routing High-Value B2B Prospects to Your Personal Attention

While your AI agent can handle a large portion of the B2B inquiry process, the highest-value prospects deserve your personal attention for the final steps. Configure your AI agent to identify and escalate these conversations to you: when a prospect indicates volume above a certain threshold, or when they are ready to discuss specific custom terms, the AI agent flags the conversation and lets them know you will be in touch personally.

This hybrid approach — AI agent for initial qualification, you for the close — ensures no wholesale opportunity falls through the cracks while still giving your most valuable prospects the personal touch that large B2B relationships require.
