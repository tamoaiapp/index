The Subscription Fatigue Problem

The SaaS model has transformed business software — and not always for the better, from the buyer's perspective. Every feature you need now comes with a monthly fee. By the time you add up all your subscriptions, you might be spending $500–$1,000/month on software before you've paid for a single physical input.

For small businesses with tight margins, this recurring cost burden is real. Every new subscription has to pass a strict ROI test, and many tools that would otherwise be valuable don't make the cut because the ongoing cost doesn't justify itself.

Why Most AI Agents Require a Subscription

The subscription model for AI agents is a consequence of cloud infrastructure costs. When an AI agent processes your customer messages on remote servers (OpenAI's, Google's, or a hosting provider's), there's a real per-message cost the provider needs to recover. Monthly subscriptions are how they do it.

This isn't a scam or a bad faith business model — it reflects genuine infrastructure costs. But it does mean your options were limited until recently.
