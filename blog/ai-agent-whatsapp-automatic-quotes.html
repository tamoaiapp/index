An AI agent changes this workflow dramatically. By handling the quote request conversation — gathering the necessary details, calculating the estimate based on your pricing structure, and sending a professional quote — your AI agent can dramatically reduce the time you spend on this essential but repetitive task.

Imagine a customer messages your WhatsApp: "Hi, I need to know how much it would cost to paint my living room." Without an AI agent, you either respond immediately (interrupting whatever you were doing) or you respond later (risking losing the customer to a competitor who replied faster).

With an AI agent, the response is immediate: "Hi! Happy to give you a quote. A few quick questions: What are the approximate dimensions of the room? Are we painting just the walls, or walls and ceiling? Do you have a preferred finish (matte, satin, gloss)? And is there any prep work needed, like filling cracks or removing old wallpaper?"

The customer answers. The AI agent gathers all the information, applies your pricing formula, and sends back: "Based on what you've shared, the estimate for painting your living room is between $X and $Y. This includes [specific inclusions]. We can confirm the exact price after a quick site visit if you'd like. Want me to schedule a time?"

The entire exchange happens automatically. You wake up to a log of quote conversations, each pre-qualified and ready for your review or direct confirmation.

Setting Up Your Pricing Logic

The key to making automatic quotes work is defining your pricing logic clearly in your TamoWork configuration. This does not require complex formulas — it requires clear descriptions of how you price your work that the AI agent can reference.

For a cleaning service, this might be: "We charge $X per hour. A standard apartment takes 2-3 hours. A house with more than 3 bedrooms typically takes 4-5 hours. Deep cleaning adds 50% to the standard rate."

For a custom product business: "Custom orders start at $Y for a standard design. Complexity upgrades (multiple colors, fine detail, larger size) add $Z each. Rush orders (under 5 days) add a 20% fee."

The AI agent uses this information to calculate accurate estimates based on the customer's specific requirements. It will not provide an exact penny-perfect quote for every situation — it provides a professional range with clear inclusions and conditions, which is exactly what most service quoting conversations need.

The Information-Gathering Conversation

Good quotes require accurate information. Your AI agent gathers this through a structured but natural conversation, asking the right questions in a logical order without overwhelming the customer with a long form. The key is to ask the minimum number of questions needed to provide a useful estimate, and to make each question feel conversational rather than interrogatory.

Configure your AI agent with the specific information you need to quote your services accurately. Think about the questions you ask every new customer and build those into the AI agent's discovery flow. Over time, you may find that certain questions consistently lead to additional information that improves quote accuracy — add these to your configuration as you learn.

Quote Follow-Up: Closing More Estimates

Most businesses send a quote and then wait. If the customer does not respond, the quote dies. An AI agent can be configured to follow up automatically after a defined period — typically 24 to 48 hours: "Just checking in — did you get a chance to review the quote I sent? Happy to answer any questions or adjust the scope if needed."

This single follow-up message can convert a significant percentage of non-responding prospects into confirmed clients. Studies across service industries consistently show that 50% or more of first-time customers need at least one follow-up before deciding. An AI agent makes sure every prospect gets followed up with, without requiring you to remember who to contact or when.

When to Involve Yourself

There are situations where a fully automated quote is not appropriate — large projects with significant complexity, requests with unusual requirements that fall outside your standard pricing, or clients who are explicitly asking for a personal meeting before committing. Your AI agent should be configured to recognize these situations and route them to you for personal handling.

The typical rule is: if the AI agent can provide a useful and accurate estimate based on the information gathered, it does so. If the situation is too complex or unclear for an accurate automated estimate, it acknowledges this and offers to connect the customer with you directly. This hybrid approach ensures automation handles the majority of routine quote requests while protecting the quality of your more complex proposals.
