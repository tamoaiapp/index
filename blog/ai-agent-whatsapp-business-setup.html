The Context Configuration: The Most Important Step

The quality of your AI agent's responses depends entirely on the quality of the context you provide during setup. Think of it as writing a comprehensive briefing document for a new employee. The more specific and complete your briefing, the better the employee performs.

Key information to include in your AI agent context:

  • What you sell — product names, descriptions, prices, variants, and availability
  • How customers buy — your ordering process, payment methods, and checkout flow
  • Delivery information — shipping options, timelines, and costs
  • Return and exchange policy
  • Common questions and your standard answers
  • Your tone — how formal or casual you want the AI agent to sound
  • Business hours — so the AI agent can let customers know when to expect personal follow-up for complex issues

Testing Before Going Live

Before letting your AI agent handle real customer conversations unsupervised, spend some time testing it. Send test messages that mimic real customer questions and review the responses. This helps you identify gaps in your context configuration and refine the AI agent's behavior before customers experience it.

Common things to test:

  • Price inquiries — does it respond correctly?
  • Complaint scenarios — does it handle them with the right tone?
  • Out-of-scope questions — does it appropriately acknowledge limits and offer to escalate?
  • Ordering process questions — does it guide customers correctly?
