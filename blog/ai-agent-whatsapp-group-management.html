Before discussing how an AI agent manages groups, it is worth understanding the different types of WhatsApp groups that businesses typically run:

Customer community groups

Groups where customers can talk to each other and to you, share their experiences with your products, ask questions, and form connections around shared interests. These are high-value but require active moderation and engagement to thrive.

VIP or loyalty groups

Exclusive groups for your best customers, where they receive early access to new products, special deals, and direct communication with you. The exclusivity is the value proposition, and the content needs to be genuinely exclusive to maintain it.

Announcement-only groups

Groups where only admins can post, used for one-way communication: flash sale alerts, new product launches, event notifications. These are lower-maintenance but still require consistent content to keep members from leaving.

Service-specific groups

Groups for customers of a particular service — a fitness class, a workshop series, a subscription box — where members share the group context and have relevant questions in common.

The Challenge of Group AI Agent Etiquette

Groups require a slightly different AI agent approach than one-on-one DMs. In a group, every message is visible to all members, which means the AI agent needs to be particularly thoughtful about tone and relevance. A few guidelines for effective group AI agent behavior:

  • Do not over-post — The AI agent should respond when directly asked or when a question goes unanswered, not automatically comment on every message
  • Keep responses concise — Long messages in group contexts are less likely to be read fully; the AI agent should answer questions clearly but briefly
  • Direct detailed inquiries to DMs — Complex or personal questions are better handled privately: "Great question — I'll send you more details in a DM!"
  • Avoid repetitive welcome messages — If the same new members join in quick succession, the AI agent should welcome them together rather than individually to avoid spam
