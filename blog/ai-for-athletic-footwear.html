Pronation and Foot Type Guidance

Pronation is one of the most important factors in running shoe selection — and one of the most commonly misunderstood. Your AI employee can explain the basics: "Overpronators need shoes with stability or motion control features to prevent the foot from rolling inward excessively. Our ASICS GT-2000 series is specifically designed for this and is one of our most recommended models for overpronators."

This kind of specific, expert guidance positions your store as a genuine specialist rather than just a product catalog — and that expertise drives purchases and referrals from satisfied customers who finally found the right shoe.

Activity Matching

Different athletic activities require fundamentally different shoe designs. A road running shoe has different cushioning and traction than a trail shoe. A court shoe for tennis has lateral stability features that a running shoe doesn't need. A HIIT cross-training shoe needs to handle multiple movement planes that a dedicated running shoe isn't designed for.

Your AI employee can match every customer to the right type of shoe for their activity before getting into specific model recommendations — ensuring the foundation of every recommendation is correct.

Comparing to Shoes Customers Already Know

Experienced athletic shoe buyers often reference their current shoe when asking for recommendations: "I've been wearing the Nike Pegasus for three years and love it — what's similar in your range?" Your AI employee can handle these comparison questions if you equip it with comparison notes — understanding the characteristics of popular shoes and identifying which of your offerings best matches that profile.
