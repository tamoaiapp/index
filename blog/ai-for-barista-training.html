How to Set Up Your Barista School's AI Employee

Define your courses clearly

Document each course offering: skill level, curriculum topics, session format, duration, class size, schedule, pricing, and certification details. If you offer individual sessions in addition to full courses, include those as well.

Speak to career outcomes

Many students are considering barista training as a genuine career move. Configure your AI employee to speak to job placement rates, industry partnerships, and the career trajectory available after completing your program. This turns an interest inquiry into a career conversation — much higher conversion.

Sell the experience

Barista training is sensory and hands-on — something most training schools don't communicate well through text alone. Configure your AI employee to describe the experience: the smell of freshly ground coffee, the satisfaction of pulling a perfect shot, the camaraderie of a small cohort. Emotional language drives enrollment decisions.

Use scarcity honestly

If your next cohort has only 2 spots left, your AI employee should say so. Truthful scarcity creates real urgency and accelerates decision-making without manipulation.
