Craft breweries are community businesses at their core. Your taproom is a gathering place. Your regulars are passionate advocates who recommend you to everyone they know. This community is your greatest asset — and it needs to be nurtured through consistent, enthusiastic engagement that most small brewery teams simply don't have time for.

Speed and responsiveness are the difference between a thriving brewery community and a stagnant one.

Craft breweries generate a distinctive mix of customer communications that spans several different categories:

  • Taproom logistics: Hours, location, parking, dog-friendly policies, reservation requirements
  • Current tap list: What's on tap right now, ABV, flavor profiles, what's new
  • Beer questions: How is this style made? What food does it pair with? Is this a seasonal or year-round release?
  • Merchandise: What gear is available? Sizes, prices, can it be shipped?
  • Events: Upcoming events, trivia nights, live music, release parties
  • Private events: Booking the taproom for private functions, capacity, catering options
  • Crowler and growler fill inquiries: Available beers for fills, pricing, hours for fills

Each category requires knowledgeable, enthusiastic answers. An AI employee handles all of them simultaneously, at any hour.

Setting Up Your Brewery AI Employee

  1. Build your current tap list with tasting notes. Style, ABV, IBU, flavor profile, food pairing suggestions. Update whenever the list changes.
  2. Catalog your merchandise. Every item, size, price, and shipping options. Include what's in stock.
  3. Document your taproom information. Hours, location, policies (dogs, children, outside food), reservation requirements.
  4. Create your event calendar entry. Upcoming events with dates, descriptions, and any reservation requirements.
  5. Define your private event process. Capacity, catering options, pricing, minimum spend, booking process.
