What an AI Employee Handles for Cardiology

An AI employee is not a replacement for medical advice — it's a communication layer that handles the administrative and informational side of patient contact. For a cardiology practice, this includes:

  • Service inquiries: What types of cardiac conditions do you treat? Do you offer stress tests? What's an echocardiogram?
  • Appointment requests: Collecting patient name, reason for visit, and preferred appointment time, then flagging it for your scheduling team.
  • Referral questions: Do you accept referrals from GPs? What information do you need to schedule a referred patient?
  • Location and hours: Standard information patients always ask about.
  • Insurance questions: Which plans do you accept? Directing patients to call for specifics.
  • Post-consultation follow-up questions: Patients who just had an appointment with a non-urgent question can get helpful, informative responses.

Handling High-Stakes Communications Appropriately

One concern cardiologists often have about automation is whether the AI might handle sensitive medical inquiries inappropriately. This is a valid concern — and TamoWork addresses it through proper configuration.

You set clear boundaries for your AI employee. You define what it answers and what it escalates. For example:

  • Questions about chest pain, emergency symptoms, or urgent medical issues — the AI immediately directs the patient to call emergency services or your urgent line.
  • Requests for medical advice — the AI explains it cannot provide medical opinions and recommends booking a consultation.
  • Administrative questions — handled fully and automatically.

This creates a responsible automation setup that helps without overstepping.
