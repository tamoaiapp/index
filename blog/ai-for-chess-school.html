Setting Up Your Chess School's AI Employee

Write your program overview

Document every class you offer: beginner, intermediate, and advanced groups; age ranges for each; session length; days and times; class sizes; teaching methodology; and pricing. Include information about your competition program — which tournaments you attend, what rating system you use, and how students progress.

Address the beginner anxiety

Many parents hesitate because they worry their child is "starting too late" or that chess is "too complicated" for young kids. Train your AI employee to proactively address these concerns with reassurance and specific examples of how quickly children pick up the game in your program.

Highlight cognitive benefits

Parents often respond strongly to the documented benefits of chess for children — improved concentration, problem-solving, math performance, and emotional regulation. Configure your AI employee to mention these when appropriate, reinforcing the value of enrollment.

Make booking effortless

Every conversation should funnel toward a specific next action: booking a trial class, downloading an enrollment form, or visiting your registration page. Configure your AI employee to always end with a clear, easy call to action.
