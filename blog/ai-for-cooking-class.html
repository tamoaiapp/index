Cooking class instructors are some of the most creative content creators on Instagram. A stunning video of handmade pasta being pulled, a timelapse of a cake being decorated, a short clip of a perfectly seared steak — these posts generate comments and DMs full of enthusiasm and interest. "How can I learn this?" "Do you offer classes?" "How much is it?" These are buying signals, and how fast you respond to them determines whether your next class fills up or not.

The challenge is that you're often in the kitchen — teaching, prepping, or creating content — when these messages arrive. An AI employee handles these inquiries the moment they appear, keeping that excitement alive and turning it into bookings.

Why Cooking Class Bookings Fall Through

Culinary education is an experience-driven purchase. People book cooking classes because they're excited about learning something specific — how to make their grandmother's recipe, how to impress guests with a gourmet dinner, how to master sourdough bread, or how to cook professionally. That excitement peaks at the moment they see your content. If they message you and don't hear back for hours, the excitement fades and the booking doesn't happen.

Additionally, cooking classes often have limited spots. When a potential student hears back too late and finds the class is full, they're disappointed and less likely to try again. An AI employee captures bookings in real time, filling spots before they're missed.

What Your AI Employee Handles

Cuisine Types and Class Themes

Every cooking instructor has specialties. Your AI employee can instantly tell prospects what types of cuisine you teach — Italian, Japanese, French pastry, vegan cooking, barbecue, bread making, or whatever your specialty is. It can describe each class theme clearly, helping prospects find the class that matches their interest.

Skill Levels and Prerequisites

Some classes are designed for beginners with no cooking experience; others are for home cooks who want to advance their skills. Your AI employee can explain the skill level for each class, whether any prior experience is expected, and how to choose the right class for someone's current ability.

Group vs. Private Classes

Many cooking instructors offer both group sessions and private lessons. Your AI employee can explain the format of each option — group size, interaction level, hands-on versus demonstration, cost difference — helping customers choose the experience that fits their goals.

Pricing and Inclusions

Cooking class pricing varies widely depending on ingredients, format, and duration. Your AI employee can communicate exactly what's included in the price — ingredients, recipes to take home, wine or beverages, aprons, certificates — and what the cancellation policy is for booked classes.
