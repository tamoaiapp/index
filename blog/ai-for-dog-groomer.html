AI for Dog Groomers: Book More Dogs with an AI Employee

🗓 March 22, 2026⏱ 8 min read

Dog grooming is hands-on work. The moment you start on a dog — bathing, drying, cutting, finishing — your phone stays in your pocket. And that is exactly when Instagram messages about availability, pricing, and breed-specific services start piling up. By the time you finish a three-hour Goldendoodle and check your phone, five potential clients have sent DMs. One or two have already found someone else.

For independent dog groomers and small grooming shops, Instagram is the primary way new clients discover your work. Those "before and after" photos are your portfolio, and when they perform well, your inbox fills up fast. The question is: who is answering it?

The Dog Grooming Booking Problem

Dog grooming inquiries are highly predictable. Pet owners want to know: Do you work with my breed? How much does it cost? How long does the appointment take? When are you next available? Do you do puppy's first groom? Can you handle an anxious dog?

These questions are simple to answer — you have answered them hundreds of times. But answering them manually, every day, across every platform, while also grooming dogs full-time is genuinely unsustainable. And yet every unanswered message is a booking you did not get.

There is a better way: an AI employee that handles the inbox while you handle the dogs.

What an AI Employee Does for Dog Groomers

TamoWork is a free tool that connects to your Instagram account and automatically replies to every DM and comment. It uses a local AI model running on your own computer — no monthly subscription, no external server, no API fees. It runs quietly in the background while you work.

For dog groomers, the AI employee handles:

  • Breed-specific pricing — Pricing by size and coat type, with specific examples for common breeds. "A standard Labrador bath and trim is $65. A full Poodle groom with scissoring is $90–$110 depending on coat condition."
  • Service menu — Bath and brush, full groom, puppy's first groom, deshedding treatment, teeth brushing, nail grinding, anal gland expression — whatever you offer, described clearly.
  • Availability — How far out you are booking, how to get on the waitlist, whether you take walk-ins or appointment only.
  • Anxious dog handling — Whether you have experience with anxious or reactive dogs, how you approach nervous clients, and any limitations on dogs you can accommodate.
  • Matted dogs — Your policy on severely matted coats, whether you shave versus dematting, what owners can expect.
  • Puppy first groom — What the first groom experience looks like, how long it takes, how to prepare the puppy beforehand.
