Fabric stores have some of the most engaged and detail-oriented customers in retail. Sewists, quilters, dressmakers, and costume designers know exactly what they want — the right weight, the right content, the right width, the right color. When they find a fabric that excites them on your Instagram feed, they have questions, and they want answers now while the inspiration is fresh.

Managing these inquiries manually takes significant time. A single fabric post on Instagram can generate dozens of comments asking about price per yard, fabric content, stretch percentage, and whether you ship. WhatsApp is often even more active — customers who've bought from you before send messages asking about new arrivals and restocks throughout the week.

Fabric and sewing supply stores deal with highly specific customer questions that vary by product. Unlike clothing retail where questions tend to be about size and fit, fabric customers ask about material properties that directly affect how they'll use it:

  • What's the fabric content of this print?
  • How much stretch does this jersey have?
  • Is this fabric pre-washed?
  • How many yards do I need for a [specific garment] in [size]?
  • Do you have this in other colorways?
  • Is this suitable for outdoor/upholstery/children's wear use?
  • Do you sell by the half yard?
  • Can I order swatches before buying?

These questions require either product knowledge or a reference to your product specifications. An AI employee configured with your fabric descriptions can handle them accurately and instantly.
