B2B wholesale inquiries have a fundamentally different character than retail consumer inquiries. Retailers asking about your wholesale terms are evaluating you as a business partner, not just a product source. They need:

  • MOQ clarity: What's the minimum order quantity per style? Per size? Per total order?
  • Pricing tiers: What are your price breaks at different volume levels? Can they get price sheets?
  • Size grading: Are you able to supply size-graded packs, or do they need to specify individual sizes?
  • Lead times: How far in advance do they need to order for seasonal stock? What's the production lead time?
  • Sampling: Is sampling available before a bulk order? What are the sample terms?
  • Payment terms: What are your payment terms — upfront, net 30, deposit-based?
  • Exclusivity: Are any styles or designs available exclusively in certain regions?

Your AI employee handles all of these with the professionalism that B2B buyers expect — and the speed that competitive wholesale markets demand.
