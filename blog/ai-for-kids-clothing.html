Age Sizing: The Kids' Clothing Puzzle

Age-based sizing in children's clothing is notoriously inconsistent. A "4-year" size in one brand might correspond to a "5-6 year" in another. Parents have been burned by this before, and they're cautious. Your AI employee can provide the actual measurements for each size — chest width, length, inseam — so parents can compare directly to their child's measurements rather than guessing by age label.

When a parent messages "My daughter just turned 4 but she's on the taller side — around 105cm. What size should I order?", your AI employee can respond with specific guidance: "At 105cm, your daughter would likely fit our 4-5 age range best. That size has a chest width of 56cm and a length of 58cm. If she's still growing quickly, our 5-6 has a bit more room to grow into."

That kind of precise, helpful response builds immediate trust — and trust is what converts parents into loyal repeat customers.
