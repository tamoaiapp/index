And they often have limited experience shopping for maternity wear, which means they need more guidance than the average fashion shopper.

A maternity wear store that provides thoughtful, knowledgeable, compassionate customer service through its AI digital employee builds customer relationships during one of the most memorable periods of a person's life — and those relationships often lead to referrals to every other expecting parent they know.

Expecting customers come to maternity stores with questions that require genuine understanding of how pregnancy affects sizing and comfort:

  • Trimester-appropriate sizing: "I'm 16 weeks — should I size up now or wait?" The answer depends on how they're carrying and their pre-pregnancy size.
  • Fabric comfort: "Is this fabric breathable? I've been running hot all the time." Fabric breathability is critical during pregnancy when body temperature runs higher.
  • Future fit: "Will this still fit me in my third trimester?" Understanding which styles accommodate significant growth is key for customers investing in maternity pieces.
  • Post-partum wearability: "Can I still wear this after the baby arrives?" Many customers want pieces that work beyond pregnancy.
  • Breastfeeding compatibility: For nursing clothing, whether garments have nursing access and how it works.

Each of these questions requires genuine knowledge of your specific products and how pregnancy affects the shopping experience.

Trimester-Based Sizing Guidance

Maternity sizing doesn't work like standard fashion sizing. Most maternity brands size by pre-pregnancy size — so someone who normally wears a medium should order a medium in maternity wear, and the garment is designed to accommodate pregnancy growth at each trimester. But nuances matter: how much stretch a fabric has, how adjustable the waistband is, whether the style is designed for early or late pregnancy.

Your AI employee can guide customers through this: "At 16 weeks, we recommend ordering your pre-pregnancy size. Our jersey styles have excellent stretch and will accommodate you through your third trimester. If you're carrying particularly large or expecting twins, we'd recommend sizing up." That guidance removes the anxiety of making the wrong choice.

Fabric and Comfort Information

Temperature regulation is a common pregnancy experience — many expecting customers run warmer than usual, especially in the second and third trimester. Fabric breathability, moisture-wicking properties, and the absence of tight elastic waistbands are all critical comfort factors. Your AI employee can highlight these features for each garment, helping customers find pieces that will actually be comfortable to wear, not just look good in photos.

The Post-Partum Question

Many customers making maternity purchases think about value — they're buying clothing they'll potentially only wear for a few months, so pieces that transition to post-partum or nursing wear have significant appeal. Your AI employee can identify these pieces and communicate their versatility, helping customers justify the purchase and increasing the perceived value of what you sell.
