The nail industry is dominated by independents and small studios. Unlike chains with dedicated front-desk staff, most nail techs are doing the work and the admin simultaneously. There is no back office fielding inquiries while you shape acrylics — it is just you.

The questions that hit your inbox are almost always the same: "How much for a full set?" "Do you do gel polish?" "Can you recreate this design?" "Are you taking bookings next week?" "Do you have a cancellation policy?" Individually, these take 30 seconds to answer. Collectively, across a week, they represent hours of context-switching that pulls your focus from the work that actually pays your bills.

An AI employee handles the repetitive end of this entirely, so you can stay present with clients and let Instagram work for you passively.

Handling Design Requests with AI

One area nail salon owners sometimes worry about is design inquiries — clients who send reference images asking "can you do this?" The AI employee cannot see images, so it cannot evaluate a specific design request. But it can be set up to respond intelligently: acknowledging the inquiry, explaining that custom design quotes require reviewing the reference, and inviting the client to send the image for a proper consultation or to book a design appointment.

This keeps the conversation moving without overpromising, and it positions you as thorough and professional rather than dismissive. The client feels heard, and you get the inquiry into a trackable booking flow.

Managing Waitlists and Last-Minute Slots

If you maintain a waitlist or occasionally post last-minute openings on Instagram, TamoWork is especially valuable. When a cancellation slot post generates thirty DMs in ten minutes, the AI employee can respond to all thirty simultaneously — first come, first served — with instructions on how to confirm the booking. You avoid the chaos of manually triaging a flood of identical messages.
