AI for Occupational Therapists: Respond to Clients 24/7

🗓 March 22, 2026⏱ 7 min read

Occupational therapists help people regain independence — with their hands, their mobility, their daily routines. It's intensive, skilled, and deeply human work. But like every healthcare professional in private practice, OTs face the same administrative challenge: client inquiries come in at all hours, and responding manually to every Instagram DM and WhatsApp message is simply not sustainable.

Whether you specialize in pediatric OT, hand therapy, neurological rehabilitation, or workplace ergonomics, new clients are searching for your services online. When they find you on Instagram and reach out, they expect a response. An AI employee gives them one — instantly, professionally, and without any effort from you.

Who Is Reaching Out to OT Practices on Social Media?

The clients and families reaching out to occupational therapists through Instagram and WhatsApp span a wide range:

  • Parents of children with sensory processing disorders or developmental delays
  • Adults recovering from hand injuries or surgeries seeking hand therapy
  • Families of stroke patients looking for rehabilitation support
  • HR departments inquiring about workplace ergonomics assessments
  • Elderly individuals or their families seeking help with daily living activities

Each of these groups has different needs and different questions. But almost all of them start with the same basic inquiries: do you treat my condition, what does a session involve, how do I book, what does it cost?

What Your AI Employee Handles Automatically

An AI employee configured for your OT practice handles the full range of initial inquiries:

  • Condition-specific questions: "Do you work with children who have sensory issues?" — the AI confirms and describes your approach.
  • Booking requests: It collects the client's name, the type of therapy needed, and their preferred contact method, queuing it for your follow-up.
  • Service explanations: What is occupational therapy? How many sessions will I need? What happens in a first assessment?
  • Insurance and pricing: Which plans you accept, general session rates, and how to verify coverage.
  • Telehealth options: Whether you offer virtual OT consultations.
  • Referral questions: Do you need a referral? What paperwork should they bring?
