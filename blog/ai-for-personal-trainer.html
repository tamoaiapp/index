Personal trainers are their own brand. Your Instagram is your portfolio, your proof of concept, and your primary lead generation tool. Personal training is a high-consideration purchase. Clients are not just buying sessions — they are trusting someone with their body, their health, and often a significant financial commitment. They do research before reaching out, and when they do reach out, they have specific questions: What is your training style? Do you do online coaching or only in-person? How much does it cost for three months? What results can I realistically expect?

These questions are the beginning of the sales conversation. Answering them well — quickly, confidently, with enough specificity to demonstrate competence — moves the conversation toward a free consultation or a signed contract. Letting them sit unanswered kills the momentum entirely.

Most personal trainers are either training clients, recovering from training clients, or creating content to attract more clients. Finding time to monitor DMs throughout the day is a genuine challenge — and yet those DMs are where revenue originates.
