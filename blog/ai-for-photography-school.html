Setting Up TamoWork for Your Photography School

Map out all your courses

Document each course you offer: title, skill level, topic focus, format (in-person/online), duration, session frequency, equipment requirements, pricing, start dates, and available seats. The more specific your knowledge base, the more precisely your AI employee can answer questions.

Sell the transformation, not just the details

Configure your AI employee to speak to what students will be able to do after your course — not just the logistics. "After 8 weeks, you'll be able to shoot professional-quality portraits and edit them to a client-ready standard" is more compelling than "the course is 8 weeks long."

Manage enrollment urgency authentically

If your courses fill up (and they should), your AI employee can mention remaining spots truthfully. Scarcity that's real creates legitimate urgency without manipulation. Configure your AI employee to update available seat counts as enrollments come in.

Handle portfolio and admission questions

If some courses require a portfolio review or have prerequisites, make sure your AI employee knows this and can walk applicants through the submission process smoothly.
