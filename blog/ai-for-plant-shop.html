Plant shops have a uniquely passionate customer base. Plant parents — as they affectionately call themselves — are enthusiastic, engaged, and always looking for their next green addition. Instagram is the natural home for plant retail. What Your AI Employee Handles

Plant Care Questions

Care questions are the lifeblood of plant retail customer service. "How much light does a fiddle leaf fig need?" "How often should I water my cactus in winter?" "What soil mix works best for succulents?" Your AI employee can answer these questions with accurate, helpful care guidance — positioning your shop as a trusted educational resource, not just a seller.

Species and Availability

Plant enthusiasts often search for specific species or varieties. Your AI employee can confirm what plants you currently carry, when certain species are expected to be available, and whether you take waitlists for rare plants. This keeps collectors engaged with your shop even when their wish-list plant isn't currently in stock.

Pricing and Size Options

Plants come in multiple sizes at different price points. Your AI employee can share pricing for different pot sizes, explain what determines the price difference, and help customers decide which size fits their space and budget best.

Delivery and Shipping

Plant delivery has unique considerations — handling, temperature sensitivity, packaging. Your AI employee can explain your delivery area, how plants are packaged to survive transit, delivery timeframes, and any guarantees you offer if a plant arrives damaged. This transparency addresses the biggest concern that holds plant buyers back from online purchases.
