Robotics schools attract two distinct audiences: parents of children (looking for STEM enrichment), and teenagers or young adults exploring engineering and computer science as career paths. Both groups have high intent when they reach out, and both groups are time-sensitive in their decisions — especially when registration deadlines or competition season approaches.

The questions they ask follow predictable patterns:

From parents:

  • What age does my child need to be to join?
  • Does my child need any programming experience?
  • What platform or system do you use — LEGO, Arduino, VEX?
  • Do you prepare students for competitions?
  • How many students are in each class?
  • What are the days and times available?
  • How much does enrollment cost?

From older students:

  • Do you teach advanced robotics concepts like computer vision or autonomous systems?
  • Are there opportunities to compete at regional or national level?
  • Do instructors have industry backgrounds?
  • Can I get a certificate that helps with college applications?

These questions are answerable. They don't require custom thinking every time — they require accurate information delivered fast. That's exactly what an AI employee does.
