AI for Speech Therapists: Automate Client Communications

🗓 March 22, 2026⏱ 7 min read

Speech therapists work in one of the most hands-on, client-focused professions in healthcare. Whether you work with children who have language delays, adults recovering from strokes, or clients managing stuttering or swallowing disorders, your attention belongs entirely in the therapy room — not on your phone, managing Instagram DMs and WhatsApp inquiries between sessions.

Yet those inquiries represent your practice growth. Parents searching for speech therapy for their child, adults looking for stuttering treatment, families dealing with a loved one's post-stroke recovery — they all turn to social media to find therapists and make first contact. If your practice doesn't respond quickly, that potential client books elsewhere.

The Challenge of Solo and Small Group Practices

Most speech therapists operate in small practices — sometimes solo. There's no receptionist dedicated to monitoring Instagram. Responding to messages happens in gaps between sessions, which means delays of hours or even days. By that time, many prospective clients have already committed to another provider.

An AI employee solves this problem without adding headcount. It monitors your channels 24/7 and provides instant, intelligent responses based on the information you've given it about your practice.

Common Inquiries Your AI Employee Can Handle

For speech therapy practices, the most frequent social media inquiries include:

  • "My 3-year-old isn't talking yet — do you work with toddlers?" — The AI confirms your age range and describes your approach.
  • "Do you treat adults with aphasia?" — The AI explains your adult services and how to book an assessment.
  • "How many sessions does speech therapy typically take?" — The AI gives a helpful general answer and recommends a consultation for specifics.
  • "Do you accept insurance?" — It lists your accepted plans and directs them to call for verification.
  • "Do you offer online speech therapy?" — It explains your telehealth options.
  • "What's your availability for new clients?" — It collects their details and notes them for your follow-up.
