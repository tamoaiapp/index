AI for Sushi Restaurants: Handle Orders and Allergen Questions

🗓 March 22, 2026⏱ 8 min read

Sushi restaurants attract customers who often have specific and serious dietary considerations. Fish allergies. Shellfish allergies. Sesame allergies — a particularly common concern given how prominently sesame appears in Japanese cuisine. Customers who are pregnant and need to know which rolls are safe. Customers who are vegan and want to know which options don't contain fish or eggs.

These are not casual questions. They require accurate, confident answers. When a customer with a shellfish allergy asks "Does your spicy tuna roll contain any shellfish?" the answer needs to be right. An AI employee configured with complete, verified allergen information gives accurate answers every time — without the variation that comes from different staff members answering the same question differently.

Beyond allergens, sushi restaurants face all the same communication challenges as any restaurant: menu questions, reservation requests, delivery logistics, and the constant flow of Instagram engagement that needs to be managed professionally.

Sushi menus are complex. A typical sushi restaurant might have 50–80 menu items, each with multiple ingredients, multiple potential allergens, and varying preparation methods. Customers frequently have questions about specific items before ordering — both for dietary safety and because they want to understand what they're eating.

During service, your sushi chefs are fully focused on precision preparation. The front of house team is managing tables, taking orders, and coordinating the dining experience. Nobody has time to stop and answer a detailed WhatsApp inquiry about whether the dragon roll contains sesame seeds. But that customer deserves an answer — and quickly.

What an AI Employee Handles for Sushi Restaurants

Complete Allergen Database

Your AI employee maintains a complete allergen profile for every menu item. Fish, shellfish, sesame, soy (found in soy sauce and many condiments), gluten, eggs, dairy — all mapped to specific menu items. When a customer asks "I'm allergic to sesame — what can I safely order?" they receive a clear, confident list of safe options based on your verified ingredient data.

Menu Navigation and Recommendations

Customers unfamiliar with Japanese cuisine often want guidance. "What's the difference between a California roll and a spider roll?" "What would you recommend for someone who's never had sushi before?" Your AI employee can provide knowledgeable, enthusiastic answers that help first-time customers navigate your menu and feel comfortable ordering.

Omakase Bookings

If you offer omakase — a chef's tasting menu — bookings require advance planning and specific information: party size, dietary restrictions, budget range, and date. Your AI employee can begin the booking conversation immediately when a customer expresses interest, collecting all necessary details before handing off to you for confirmation.

Delivery Order Management

Sushi delivery requires extra attention to detail — packing, timing, temperature considerations. Your AI employee collects complete delivery orders including specific roll selections, any modifications, delivery address and timing, and any allergen notes that need to be communicated to the kitchen. Clean, complete orders reduce errors that are especially costly in a restaurant where wrong ingredients can have health consequences.

Setting Up Your Sushi Restaurant AI Employee

  1. Build your complete allergen database first. This is the most critical element. Map every allergen to every menu item accurately. Have this verified by your kitchen team.
  2. Create detailed menu descriptions. Go beyond just names — describe key ingredients, flavor profiles, and what makes each item special.
  3. Define your booking process for omakase and large parties. What information do you need upfront? What's your deposit policy?
  4. Set up delivery parameters. Zones, minimums, timing, and any special instructions for sushi delivery.
  5. Configure TamoWork and test allergen scenarios thoroughly. This is the most important testing you'll do — run through every common allergy scenario and verify accuracy.
