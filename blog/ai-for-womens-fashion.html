Every woman's fashion inquiry follows a predictable pattern. Understanding that pattern is the key to automating it effectively.

The most common questions your AI employee will handle include:

  • Sizing and fit: "I'm between a small and medium, which do you recommend?" or "Does this run true to size?"
  • Body type guidance: "I have a pear-shaped body, would this dress work for me?"
  • Fabric and comfort: "Is this fabric stretchy?" or "Will this be comfortable for a long event?"
  • Styling suggestions: "What shoes would you pair with this?" or "Can I wear this to a wedding?"
  • Availability: "Do you have the sage green in a size 8?" or "When is the coral color coming back?"

Each of these questions, answered well, moves the customer toward purchase. Answered poorly or not at all, they close the sale before it starts.

Size Guides That Actually Help

The biggest friction point in women's fashion sales is sizing anxiety. Women know that sizing varies wildly between brands and even between styles within the same brand. A customer who is unsure whether to order a medium or large may abandon the purchase rather than risk a wrong fit and the hassle of returning it.

Your AI employee eliminates that anxiety by providing specific, detailed size guidance. Not just "please refer to our size chart" — but genuine advice: "Our wrap dresses tend to fit generously. If you're between sizes, we usually recommend going with your smaller size. If you prefer a more relaxed fit, go with the larger." That level of specificity reassures the customer and dramatically increases their confidence to complete the purchase.

Setting Up Your Women's Fashion AI Employee

Step 1: Build Detailed Product Profiles

For each item in your collection, document the fit type (slim, relaxed, oversized), the size range available, any sizing quirks, the fabric composition, care instructions, available colors, price, and styling suggestions. The more detailed these profiles, the more helpful your AI employee's responses will be.

Step 2: Create a Body Type Guide

One of the most impactful additions to your AI employee's knowledge is a guide that connects each item in your collection to the body types it flatters. This allows your AI employee to give personalized recommendations — "This A-line midi skirt is particularly flattering for hourglass and pear body types" — that feel genuinely helpful rather than generic.
