Common Concerns and How to Address Them

"Will customers know they're talking to AI?"

Configure your AI employee to be transparent — it's your business assistant, not an impersonator. Most customers don't mind getting an immediate, helpful automated response — they prefer it to waiting hours for a human reply. What they dislike is feeling deceived. Transparency and speed together create a better customer experience than a slow human response.

"What if the AI says something wrong?"

The AI only says what you configure it to say. If you give it accurate information about your services, pricing, and availability, it will provide accurate responses. Start conservatively — give it less information and expand as you see how it performs. Review conversations regularly and update your configuration when you spot gaps.

"My business is too complex for automation"

The AI employee doesn't need to handle everything — it just needs to handle first contact well. Even for highly complex services, the first message is almost always one of a handful of predictable types: "what do you do?", "how much does it cost?", "are you available?", "I need help with X." These are entirely automatable, and handling them automatically is where most of the lead-capture value lies.
