Step 2

Set Up the WhatsApp Employee

Open TamoWork. Click "WhatsApp Employee." Scan the QR code with your phone to link your WhatsApp account. You're now ready to configure your 24/7 AI employee.

Step 3

Build a Thorough Knowledge Base

For 24/7 support to be effective, your AI employee needs comprehensive training. Go beyond basic product info. Include: your complete product/service catalog with prices, all delivery and shipping options, return and refund policy, payment methods accepted, common objections and how to handle them, and your business hours (so the AI can set expectations appropriately). Aim for at least 500 words of training data.

Step 4

Set After-Hours Response Style

Configure how the AI should communicate during off-hours. For example: acknowledge what time it is ("Good evening!"), provide all requested information, and add a note like "I'll pass your details to our team to follow up personally tomorrow morning." This sets honest expectations while still engaging the customer.

Step 5

Configure Escalation Rules

Not every message should be auto-replied to. Configure TamoWork to flag messages that contain words like "complaint," "urgent," "wrong order," or "refund." These get held for your personal review instead of receiving an automated reply. This protects customer relationships for sensitive situations.

Step 6

Enable Startup with Windows

For true 24/7 coverage, make sure TamoWork starts automatically when your PC boots. Go to Windows startup settings (press Win+R, type "shell:startup") and add a TamoWork shortcut there. Now even after a power outage or restart, your WhatsApp coverage resumes automatically.

Step 7

Review Weekly and Continuously Improve

Spend 15 minutes each week reading through the conversations your AI employee had. Identify the weakest responses and improve your training data accordingly. After 4–6 weeks of refinement, most business owners find their AI employee handles 90%+ of conversations without any input needed from them.
