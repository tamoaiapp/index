Step-by-Step: Automating WhatsApp FAQ Responses

Step 1

Audit Your Last Month of WhatsApp Messages

Before building your FAQ, do a quick audit. Scroll back through your last month of WhatsApp conversations (or as far back as you can) and write down every distinct question you received. Group similar questions together. You will likely find that 15-20 question categories cover the vast majority of what you receive. This audit is the foundation of your automation strategy.

Step 2

Write Ideal Answers for Each Question

For each question category, write the ideal complete answer — the answer you would give if you had all the time in the world to respond thoughtfully. Not the rushed answer you actually type at 6pm when you are exhausted. The ideal answer. This might be 2-3 sentences, or it might include multiple options. Write it in your natural voice, the way you talk to customers.

Step 3

Install TamoWork and Connect WhatsApp Business

Download TamoWork from tamowork.com and install it. Connect your WhatsApp Business account via the QR code link in Accounts — WhatsApp — Connect. TamoWork will begin monitoring all incoming messages. You are ready to build your FAQ library in the system.

Step 4

Enter Your FAQ into TamoWork

Go to AI Settings — FAQ. Add each question from your audit with the ideal answer you wrote in Step 2. Enter questions the way customers actually phrase them — not formal "What is your return policy?" but more like "Can I return something if I don't like it?" The AI understands natural language variations, so you do not need to enter every possible phrasing — just representative examples of each question type.

Step 5

Add Context in Your Business Profile

The FAQ handles specific questions, but your Business Profile (AI Settings — Business Profile) provides the background context the AI uses to understand and respond to questions that are not explicitly in the FAQ. Make this profile rich and specific — it is the difference between an AI that gives relevant answers and one that gives generic answers.

Step 6

Enable Auto-Reply and Set Response Rules

In WhatsApp Settings — Automation, enable automatic replies. Set a 1-3 minute response delay. Configure any specific rules you need — for example, do not auto-reply to messages from contacts in your "VIP" list (handle those personally), or do not auto-reply when a message contains the word "complaint" (flag those for personal attention).

Step 7

Test and Refine Your FAQ Responses

Send yourself test messages that match each of your FAQ categories. Evaluate each generated response: Is the information accurate? Does it fully answer the question? Does it sound like your business? Is there a clear next step for the customer? Refine your FAQ entries and business profile until every test response is excellent. Go live when you are satisfied.

Your Complete WhatsApp FAQ Checklist

Make sure your FAQ covers at minimum these categories:

  • Pricing — all products/services with current prices
  • Delivery — areas covered, timeframes, fees
  • Ordering process — step-by-step how to buy
  • Payment methods — what you accept, where to send
  • Business hours — when you are available for personal follow-up
  • Returns and refunds — your policy, clearly stated
  • Custom orders — whether you do them, how to request
  • Stock availability — how to check if something is available
  • Minimum orders — if applicable
  • Contact for urgent matters — how to reach a real person if needed

Growing Your FAQ Over Time

Your FAQ is a living document. Every week, check the reply log and look for any messages the AI handled imperfectly or any new question patterns you have not seen before. Add these to your FAQ immediately. After a few months of regular maintenance, you will have an exceptionally comprehensive FAQ that handles virtually every common message with no manual effort.
