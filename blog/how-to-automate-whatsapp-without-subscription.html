The Subscription Trap in WhatsApp Automation

Most WhatsApp automation tools are built on Meta's Business API, which charges per conversation. The platforms built on top of this API then add their own markup and monthly fees. For a business receiving 500 conversations per month, you might pay $50-200+ just for the WhatsApp messaging costs, before the platform subscription on top of that.

TamoWork bypasses this entirely. It connects to WhatsApp Business directly via the linked devices system — the same way WhatsApp Web works. No API, no per-message charges, no platform markup. The AI runs on your computer. The cost is zero.

Step-by-Step: Subscription-Free WhatsApp Automation

Step 1

Download and Install TamoWork

Visit tamowork.com and download the installer for your operating system. Run it and follow the setup wizard. TamoWork installs in about 3-5 minutes. The AI model is included — you do not need to download anything else or create any accounts on any external service. Once installed, TamoWork is ready to configure.

Step 2

Link WhatsApp Business to TamoWork

Open TamoWork. Navigate to Accounts — WhatsApp — Connect Account. A QR code appears. On your phone, open WhatsApp Business and go to Settings — Linked Devices — Link a Device. Scan the QR code. TamoWork is now connected to your WhatsApp and can read and reply to messages. The connection works even when your phone is off or its battery is dead.

Step 3

Set Up Your Business Information

In AI Settings — Business Profile, describe your business completely. Include every detail a customer might need: your products and services, prices or price ranges, how to place an order, payment options, delivery areas and estimated times, business hours, and your refund policy. Write in plain language — conversational and complete.

Step 4

Create Your Question-and-Answer Library

In AI Settings — FAQ, add question-and-answer pairs for your most common WhatsApp inquiries. Think about the last month of messages you received. What did people ask most? Create an entry for each one. Some businesses start with 10 entries and grow to 40+ over time as they discover new patterns. Start with what you know and expand as you go.

Step 5

Configure Your Response Rules

In WhatsApp Settings — Automation, enable auto-reply. Set a delay (1-3 minutes works well for WhatsApp). Define which conversations to handle automatically and which to escalate. You can set different rules for new contacts vs. existing customers, or for messages received outside business hours vs. during business hours.

Step 6

Run Tests with Real-World Questions

Use TamoWork's Test Mode to simulate real customer messages before going live. Try vague questions, price requests, complaints, and compliments. Review each generated reply for accuracy and tone. Refine your FAQ until the test results are consistently excellent. This testing phase prevents your customers from receiving any subpar automated replies.

Step 7

Launch and Do Your Daily 5-Minute Review

Enable live automation. Starting from day one, build a habit of opening TamoWork for 5 minutes every morning. Review what happened since your last check. Handle any escalated messages personally. Note any reply that could be improved and update your FAQ. After two weeks, this morning review usually drops to 2-3 minutes — just a quick scan to make sure everything is running smoothly.
