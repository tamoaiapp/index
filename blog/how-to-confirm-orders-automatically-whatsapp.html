How to Confirm Orders Automatically on WhatsApp

🗓 March 22, 2026⏱ 8 min read

For businesses that take orders via WhatsApp — food businesses, clothing stores, custom product sellers, service businesses — order confirmation is a critical but repetitive step. A customer places an order, you need to confirm the details, send a total, and provide next steps. It takes 3-5 minutes per order. Multiply by 15-20 orders a day and you are spending over an hour just on confirmations.

This guide shows you how to automate that process so customers get instant, accurate order confirmations while you focus on actually making and delivering what they ordered.

Why Order Confirmations Matter So Much

An order confirmation on WhatsApp serves multiple functions. It reassures the customer that their order was received and understood. It documents the details of what was agreed. It sets clear expectations about price and delivery. And it gives the customer something to reference if there are any questions later.

A slow or vague confirmation creates uncertainty. Customers worry: did they see my order? Did I get the price right? Is it coming? An instant, detailed confirmation eliminates that anxiety and builds trust before the order even ships.

Step-by-Step: Automating WhatsApp Order Confirmations

Step 1

Standardize Your Ordering Process

Before automating anything, write down your ideal ordering process as a step-by-step flow. What information do you need from every customer? (Name, address, product, size, quantity, delivery date?) What information do you give them back? (Total price, estimated delivery time, payment instructions?) Document this clearly — it becomes the blueprint for your automated confirmation system.

Step 2

Install TamoWork and Connect WhatsApp

Go to tamowork.com, download TamoWork for your operating system, and install it. In the app, go to Accounts — WhatsApp — Connect and scan the QR code with your WhatsApp Business app. TamoWork will now receive all incoming messages in real time.

Step 3

Write a Detailed Business and Pricing Profile

In AI Settings — Business Profile, write out your complete product catalog with prices. Include every product variant, size, and option. Include any conditions: minimum orders, available delivery days, advance notice required. This allows the AI to calculate order totals and provide accurate confirmations automatically.

Step 4

Create Order Confirmation Templates in Your FAQ

In AI Settings — FAQ, create entries that simulate the order confirmation process. For example: "Customer confirms order for 2 chocolate cakes, delivery Saturday, address in our delivery zone" → the ideal confirmation message format you want to send, including total price calculation, confirmation of delivery date, payment instructions, and any prep notes for you. The AI uses this as a template to generate real confirmations.

Step 5

Set Up an Order Detection Trigger

In TamoWork's Automation Rules, create a trigger that activates the order confirmation flow when specific order-related keywords appear in a message. Examples: "I'd like to order," "can I get," "I want to buy," "quero pedir," or "my address is." When these phrases are detected, TamoWork uses your order confirmation template to generate the appropriate reply.

Step 6

Configure Order Escalation for Complex Orders

Not every order can be confirmed automatically. For orders requiring special arrangements — large bulk orders, custom products not in your standard catalog, out-of-area deliveries — set up escalation rules that flag these for personal handling. The AI should acknowledge the order request and tell the customer you will confirm within a specific timeframe, so they are not left wondering.

Step 7

Test Your Order Flow End-to-End

Use a friend's WhatsApp to send a complete order message as a customer would. Go through the entire flow: initial inquiry, product selection, confirmation details, and payment instructions. Verify that each step produces the correct automated response. Make sure pricing is calculated correctly and that delivery expectations match your actual capabilities. Only go live once the full flow works correctly.

What a Good Automated Order Confirmation Includes

  1. Acknowledgment of the specific items ordered (with quantities and variants)
  2. Total price including any delivery fee
  3. Estimated delivery or ready date
  4. Payment instructions (where to send, how much)
  5. Your contact information for any questions
  6. A warm thank-you note
