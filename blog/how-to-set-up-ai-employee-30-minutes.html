The most common reason small business owners delay setting up automation is the assumption that it will be complicated. A full afternoon of configuration, a technical problem halfway through, something that does not quite work. This guide proves that assumption wrong.

TamoWork is designed to go from download to live in under 30 minutes for a business owner with no technical background. Here is the exact process, minute by minute, including the most common mistakes and how to avoid them.

The 30-Minute Setup Timeline

1

Minutes 0–5: Download and Install

Go to tamowork.com and click "Download free." The installer is a standard .exe (Windows) or .dmg (Mac) file. Run it like you would any other app — click through the installer, let it finish. TamoWork will automatically detect whether Ollama (the local AI engine) is already on your computer. If not, it will prompt you to install it with a single click. The Ollama installation includes downloading the AI model (~4GB), so keep your computer plugged in and connected to the internet for this part. Once this one-time download is complete, internet is no longer required for daily operation.

2

Minutes 5–10: Connect Your Channels

TamoWork's dashboard shows your available channels: Instagram and WhatsApp. Click "Connect" next to the channel you want to start with. For Instagram, you will log in through a standard Instagram authentication screen — no password is stored by TamoWork. For WhatsApp, you scan a QR code with your phone, exactly like WhatsApp Web. The connection takes under a minute once you have your phone ready. You do not need to choose both channels immediately. Many users start with one, verify it works well for their business, then add the second.

3

Minutes 10–20: Write Your Business Context

This is the most important step. TamoWork's AI employee needs to know about your business before it can reply accurately on your behalf. You fill in a form with the key information your AI worker will use in every conversation. See the template below for exactly what to include. Take your time with this section — 10 minutes of careful writing here translates directly into better replies. The AI is only as knowledgeable as what you tell it. Vague input produces vague replies; specific input produces accurate, useful replies.

4

Minutes 20–25: Test With Sample Questions

Before going live, use TamoWork's built-in test panel to ask it the questions your customers ask most often. Type in a typical message — "How much does it cost?", "Do you deliver?", "What sizes do you have?" — and read the AI's response. If a reply is missing something important or says something inaccurate, go back to your business context and add the missing detail. Test until you are satisfied that the replies sound like you and contain the right information. Most users need 2-3 small adjustments during this phase.

5

Minutes 25–30: Go Live

Toggle the "Active" switch on your dashboard. Your AI employee is now working. The next message that arrives on your connected Instagram or WhatsApp will receive an automatic reply from your AI worker. You will still see all conversations in your inbox — nothing is hidden from you. You can step in and take over any conversation at any time. The AI employee runs alongside you, not instead of you.

Business Context Template

Copy and adapt this template for your business context setup:

Business name: [Your business name]

What we sell: [Describe your products or services in 2-3 sentences. Be specific about categories, price ranges, and what makes your offering different.]

Pricing: [List your main products/services with prices. Include ranges if pricing varies. E.g., "T-shirts from $25, hoodies from $55, custom orders start at $80."]

Operating hours: [Days and hours. Include whether you reply to messages outside business hours.]

Delivery/shipping: [Do you deliver? Shipping times, costs, geographic coverage. For physical stores: address and how to visit.]

How to order: [Exact steps a customer takes to place an order with you.]

Common questions and answers: [List 5-10 questions you get asked frequently with your preferred answers. This is the most valuable section.]

Tone: [How do you want to sound? Friendly and casual? Professional? Warm and personal? Include specific phrases you use or avoid.]

What to do when uncertain: [Tell the AI what to say when a question falls outside what it knows. E.g., "If unsure, say 'Let me check on that and get back to you shortly.'"]

Common Mistakes in First Setup

  • Being too vague about products: "We sell clothes" gives the AI nothing to work with. "We sell women's streetwear — hoodies, cargo pants, oversized tees — sizes XS to 2XL, prices from $35 to $90" gives it everything it needs.
  • Forgetting to include pricing: Pricing is the most common question. If you do not include it, the AI will either guess or give a non-answer. Both are worse than a clear price range.
  • Skipping the test phase: Going live without testing means your first real customers get to experience any errors. Five minutes of testing prevents this entirely.
  • Setting tone too formally: Most small business owners communicate warmly with their customers. If you set the tone to "professional and formal," your replies will feel out of character. Match the way you actually talk.
  • Not updating after changes: When your prices change, your menu updates, or you add new products, update the business context. The AI does not know about changes you make to your business unless you tell it.

What to Expect the First Week

Days 1-2: Monitor replies closely. Read everything the AI sends and note anything that needs correction. Make small adjustments to your business context based on what you observe. This is the calibration phase.

Days 3-5: You will notice the types of questions the AI handles confidently and any areas where it still needs more context. Add any missing information. Most businesses find that 90% of replies are correct and natural by day three.

Days 5-7: You will start to feel the time you are getting back. The mental overhead of constantly monitoring your inbox will lift. Customers will receive consistent, accurate replies even when you are not at your phone. By the end of the first week, the AI employee will feel like a genuine part of how your business operates.

The setup investment is real but small. Thirty minutes today, a few small tweaks over the first few days, and then your business has an AI worker on duty 24 hours a day, seven days a week, at no cost.
