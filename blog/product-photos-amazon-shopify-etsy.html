💡 Why this matters

Most online sellers never calculate how much revenue they lose monthly because of poor product photos. If you're reading "Product Photo Requirements for Amazon, Shopify and Etsy", you're already ahead of them.

Amazon: strict white background rules

Main image: pure white background (#FFFFFF), product fills 85%+ of frame, no watermarks, no additional text or graphics. Minimum 1000px on longest side (for zoom). Recommended: 2000x2000px JPEG.

Shopify: square for consistency

Square images (1:1) at 2048x2048px for best quality. JPEG for solid products, PNG for transparent backgrounds. Consistent style across all products creates a professional storefront feel.

Etsy: lifestyle images convert better

Etsy buyers respond to lifestyle and context photos. Main image: product clearly visible, square format. Include at least one lifestyle photo showing the product in use or in a real environment.

eBay: quantity wins

eBay allows up to 24 photos per listing. Listings with 10+ photos have significantly higher sold rates. Include every angle, detail shots, and condition documentation for used items.

Frequently asked questions

❓ What image size should I use for e-commerce listings?

Minimum 1000x1000px for any marketplace. Ideal is 1200x1200px to 2000x2000px to enable quality zoom. Square format (1:1) works across all major platforms. Use JPEG for solid products, PNG when you need a transparent background.

❓ Can phone photos be good enough for product listings?

Yes, with the right techniques. Use natural window light (not yellow artificial light), a clean white background, and stabilize your phone to avoid blur. TamoWork then handles post-processing to elevate to professional standard.

❓ Is it worth hiring a photographer for e-commerce?

For catalogs with frequent turnover or more than 20 products per month, a photographer ($30-80/photo) is not scalable. AI like TamoWork covers unlimited photos for $49/month — far better ROI for medium-volume sellers.

❓ How do I prevent returns caused by photos not matching the real product?

Shoot with correct white balance (natural light or 5000K LED) to ensure accurate colors. Include texture and material detail shots. Add a size reference (ruler or hand). Never apply filters that significantly alter color.
