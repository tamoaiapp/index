Step by Step: How to Do It

Step 1

Check Your Windows Version

TamoWork works on Windows 10 (version 1909+) and Windows 11. Check your version by pressing Win+R, typing "winver," and pressing Enter. You also need at least 4GB of RAM and about 500MB of free disk space. Most PCs used for business easily meet these requirements.

Step 2

Download TamoWork for Free

Open your browser and go to tamowork.com. Click the "Download free" button. The installer (about 200MB) downloads directly to your PC. No account required, no email sign-up, no payment information needed.

Step 3

Install on Windows

Double-click the downloaded installer. If Windows Defender or SmartScreen shows a warning, click "More info" and then "Run anyway" — TamoWork is safe but may be unfamiliar to Windows security tools since it's newer software. Follow the installation prompts. The process takes about 2 minutes.

Step 4

First Launch: Load the Local AI

Open TamoWork using the desktop shortcut. On the first launch, the local AI model initializes. This takes 1–2 minutes and only happens once. After initialization, you'll see the main dashboard. Every subsequent launch is nearly instantaneous.

Step 5

Set Up the WhatsApp Employee

Click "WhatsApp Employee" on the dashboard. A QR code appears. Open WhatsApp on your phone, tap the three-dot menu or Settings icon, select "Linked Devices," then "Link a Device," and scan the QR code. WhatsApp is now connected to TamoWork on your Windows PC.

Step 6

Train the AI and Configure Automation

Fill out the business training form with your complete business information. Then configure automation settings: reply speed, escalation rules, greeting style, and which message types to handle automatically. These settings can be updated anytime — you're not locked into your initial configuration.

Step 7

Set Up Windows Startup for Continuous Coverage

For always-on WhatsApp automation, add TamoWork to Windows startup. Press Win+R, type "shell:startup," press Enter. A folder opens. Create a shortcut to TamoWork here. Now TamoWork starts automatically every time Windows boots, ensuring your WhatsApp automation never has downtime.

Important Tips

  • Prevent Windows sleep: Adjust your Power Settings so Windows doesn't sleep while TamoWork is running. Go to Settings > System > Power & Sleep and set Sleep to "Never" or a long duration.
  • Use a wired connection when possible: For reliable 24/7 WhatsApp automation, a wired Ethernet connection is more stable than WiFi, especially overnight when router reboots can occur.
  • Works on older Windows PCs: TamoWork runs well on older hardware. Even a Windows 10 PC from 2017 with 4GB RAM can handle WhatsApp automation without performance issues.
  • No Windows Server required: Unlike enterprise automation tools that require server infrastructure, TamoWork runs perfectly on a standard Windows desktop or laptop. No IT department needed.
